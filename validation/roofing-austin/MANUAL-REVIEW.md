# Manual Review

## Verdict

The system can create real value.

Across 3 Austin roofing businesses, the analysis, profile, website, ads, and outreach outputs were consistently specific enough to be useful. This is no longer "it technically works" territory. The core loop is proving that it can diagnose a business, identify a sharper angle, and produce assets that feel directionally deployable.

## Processed Businesses

- Roof Company Austin — score 83/100 — Strong Opportunity
- Austin Roofing Company — score 74/100 — Testable
- Altair Austin Roofing Company — score 89/100 — Strong Opportunity

## What Feels Strong

- Analysis quality is strong. The system is diagnosing different weaknesses per business instead of repeating one generic playbook.
- Audience understanding is much better than before. The profiles speak to storm anxiety, insurance confusion, trust fear, timing pressure, and cost sensitivity in a way that matches real roofing buyers.
- Website outputs are conversion-oriented. They consistently improve CTA specificity, trust positioning, urgency, and local framing.
- Ads feel usable. The hooks are sharper than generic "need a roofer?" copy and often include local/weather-driven context that could realistically stop the scroll.
- Outreach emails are the biggest pleasant surprise. The best ones read like a consultant who already did the work, not a templated cold email bot.

## What Feels Weak

- The generation style is still a little samey across businesses. "Storm + insurance + trust + urgency" is the right spine for roofing, but some outputs feel too templated around that formula.
- A few claims are stronger than the source material supports. Example: large review counts, guarantee details, inspection speed, or neighborhood coverage sometimes feel inferred rather than observed.
- Some website hero lines are good but not yet elite. A few are persuasive, but not all feel like the strongest possible headline for that exact business.
- Business 2 is the weakest of the 3. It's still usable, but the output leans more "strong agency copy" than "this company's unmistakable voice."
- Some outreach copy still sounds more polished than natural owner-to-owner email. Good enough to test, but a human tightening pass would help.

## Manual Checks

### Website

- Better than current site: yes, for all 3.
- Trustworthy: yes, especially Roof Company Austin and Altair.
- Risk: some trust claims should be validated before publishing live.

### Ads

- Scroll-stopping: yes, especially where the hook uses storm timing, insurance frustration, or contractor distrust.
- Real vs generic: mostly real. They are much stronger than generic AI ad copy, but still need variation testing to avoid sameness.

### Email

- Would I reply: yes to Roof Company Austin, maybe to Austin Roofing Company, maybe to Altair depending on sender credibility.
- Best email trait: specific diagnosis and low-pressure tone.
- Weakest email trait: some lines still sound a little too polished and "agency-smart."

### Profile

- Does it understand the customer: yes.
- Biggest improvement over earlier runs: customer pains are now concrete and relevant instead of broad demographic filler.

## Recommendation

Do not expand features yet.

Next move should be a refinement pass, not more product surface area:

- tighten unsupported claims in generated copy
- increase business-specific voice and differentiation
- add guardrails so inferred proof points are labeled or softened
- run the same validation on one more niche before building deploy/automation work
