# KWANUS — Copy Doctrine

## Voice

KWANUS speaks like a **trusted financial consultant** — knowledgeable, calm, direct, and empowering.

Not a salesperson. Not a robot. Not a friend. A professional guide.

## Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Onboarding | Welcoming, clear, reassuring |
| Education | Knowledgeable, patient, structured |
| Dispute guidance | Direct, factual, empowering |
| Progress updates | Encouraging, specific, honest |
| Warnings/blockers | Calm, clear, actionable |
| Funding guidance | Measured, realistic, educational |

## Writing Rules

### Do:
- Use plain language (8th grade reading level)
- Lead with what the user should do, then explain why
- Be specific ("Review 3 flagged items" not "Check your items")
- Use active voice ("You can challenge this item" not "This item can be challenged")
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Front-load important information

### Don't:
- Use jargon without explanation
- Use filler words ("just", "simply", "actually", "basically")
- Use exclamation marks (except rare celebration moments)
- Use ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Write paragraphs when a list will do
- Use fear-based language ("Your credit is at risk!")

## Compliance-Safe Patterns

### Instead of "We will fix..."
> "You have the right to challenge inaccurate information on your credit report. Here's how."

### Instead of "Guaranteed improvement..."
> "Users who follow this process typically see progress. Your results depend on your specific situation."

### Instead of "We'll dispute this for you..."
> "This educational template is ready for your review. Once you're satisfied, you can send it directly to the bureau."

### Instead of "Get approved..."
> "Strengthen your profile to improve your positioning before applying."

## UI Copy Patterns

### Headings
- Action-oriented: "Review Your Flagged Items"
- Status-oriented: "3 Disputes Active"
- Question-oriented: "Ready for the Next Step?"

### Buttons
- Primary: Clear verb + object ("Review Items", "Generate Letter", "Check Readiness")
- Secondary: "Learn More", "View Details", "Skip for Now"
- Never: "Submit", "Click Here", "Go"

### Empty States
- What this is: "This is where your active disputes will appear."
- What to do: "Upload your credit report to get started."
- Why it matters: "Tracking disputes helps you see progress over time."

### Error States
- What happened: "We couldn't process that file."
- What to do: "Try uploading a PDF or HTML version of your credit report."
- Reassurance: "Your data is safe. Nothing was changed."

### Success States
- What happened: "Your dispute letter is ready."
- What's next: "Review it, customize if needed, then send it to the bureau."

## Content Hierarchy

1. **What to do** (action)
2. **Why it matters** (context)
3. **How to do it** (instruction)
4. **What happens next** (expectation)

Always in this order. Never bury the action.
