## Product Discovery Signals

**In order of reliability:**
1. **Ad velocity**: 50+ active ad variants simultaneously on Minea/AdSpy = real spend, not testing. Cross-reference "first seen" date — under 30 days = early mover window.
2. **TikTok organic virality before paid**: Search the product on TikTok. 500K–5M organic views with no brand = paid will amplify it. #1 leading indicator.
3. **Comment sentiment**: "Where do I buy this," "I need this," "sending to [person]" = buying intent. Skeptical comments ("does this actually work?") = conversion friction, avoid.
4. **Pipiads for TikTok**: Filter by "first seen" within 7 days, sort by engagement rate. Above 8% = exceptional. 3–5% = baseline interest.
5. **Novelty delta**: Has this existed on Amazon for 3+ years? If yes, organic discovery moment has passed. Want: known problem, new form factor.

**Tools workflow:**
1. Minea (Facebook) → ads with 500+ likes, running 2+ weeks
2. Pipiads → cross-validate on TikTok
3. TikTok search → organic view velocity
4. AliExpress/CJdropshipping → order volume trend (10K+ orders with recent uptick)
5. Google Trends → confirm upward curve, not peak

**Key insight**: The same product sold with 3 different angles (pain relief vs. confidence vs. office workers) converts at wildly different rates. Angle matters more than product.

## Product Validation Criteria

| Criteria | Minimum | Ideal |
|---|---|---|
| Selling price | $25 | $39–$65 |
| COGS (landed) | Under $12 | Under $9 |
| Markup | 3x | 4–5x |
| Shipping time | Under 12 days | Under 7 days |
| Weight | Under 500g | Under 200g |

**Qualitative checklist:**
- [ ] Can you demonstrate it solving a problem in under 8 seconds on video?
- [ ] Does it have a "wow moment" — instant "I didn't know that existed"?
- [ ] Is there a clear before/after or transformation?
- [ ] Can it be explained without text overlay — pure visual?
- [ ] Does it trigger emotion: relief, desire, humor, curiosity?

**Immediate disqualifiers:**
- Fragile (high return rate)
- Requires sizing (clothing/shoes — 25–40% return rate)
- Electrical with no certifications (payment processor risk)
- Already saturated on every influencer page
- Seasonal only

**Margin math at $39 price point:**
- $8 COGS + $4 shipping + $3 processing = $24 gross margin
- Target $15–18 CPP = $6–9 net per order
- 200 orders/day = $1,200–$1,800/day net

## Store Structure

**Format decision:**
- **One-product store**: Best for high wow-factor impulse products. CVR ceiling 4–8%. Zero LTV.
- **Niche store**: Best for scaling past $50K/month. Upsells, email list value, repeat purchase.
- **General store**: Testing only. Never scale on a general store.

**High-converting product page anatomy (in order):**
1. **Headline**: Problem-focused. "Finally Sleep Through The Night" not "Cervical Pillow Pro"
2. **Hero video**: Above fold, autoplay, muted, 30–45 seconds. Shows transformation.
3. **Trust bar**: "50,000+ Sold," "Free Shipping," "30-Day Guarantee" — immediately below ATC
4. **Benefit bullets**: 3–5, outcome-focused not feature-focused
5. **Social proof block**: 20+ reviews minimum (photo reviews), import via Loox/Judge.me
6. **Guarantee section**: Full-page treatment. Risk reversal framing.
7. **FAQ section**: Answers the 5 objections killing conversion (shipping, returns, authenticity, compatibility, safety)
8. **Sticky ATC button**: Always visible on mobile

**What top 1% have that average stores don't:**
- Custom product photography (not supplier stock)
- Real reviews with faces/photos, not text-only
- Specific shipping estimate ("Arrives by [date] if ordered today")
- Post-purchase upsell (Zipify/ReConvert) adding $8–15 AOV
- Mobile page load under 2.5 seconds
- Page load score 70+ on mobile

## Ad Creative Strategy

**UGC vs. Produced:**
- UGC style: 30–40% lower CPMs, wins on trust
- Produced: works for high-ticket ($80+) and brand plays
- Best performers: UGC-style but professionally scripted — looks raw, is calculated

**Hook formulas that work:**
1. Problem agitation: "I was waking up with neck pain every morning until I found this…"
2. Skeptic-to-believer: "I thought this was a scam until my package arrived…"
3. Social proof open: "50,000 people can't be wrong about this…"
4. Demonstration cold open: Product doing the thing in frame 1, no intro
5. Pattern interrupt: Unexpected visual or sound in first frame

**30-second script structure:**
- 0–3s: Hook (visual + audio pattern interrupt)
- 3–8s: Problem identification (viewer self-identifies)
- 8–18s: Product demonstration (the wow moment)
- 18–25s: Social proof + outcome
- 25–30s: CTA + urgency

**Testing matrix:**
- Launch: 3 hooks × 3 bodies = 9 creatives minimum
- Budget: $10–20/day per ad set (ABO during testing)
- Decision window: 3 days, $50 minimum before killing
- Winner criteria: CPC under $0.80, CTR above 2%, ATC rate above 8%

**Creative refresh:** Frequency 2.5+ = fatigue. Refresh every 2–3 weeks. Top 1% have 15–20 active creatives per product, continuously retiring and launching.

## Scaling Playbook

| Phase | Budget | Structure | Target ROAS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 — Testing | $10–20/ad set (ABO) | 5–10 ad sets, broad + 3–4 interest stacks | Break-even (1.5–2x) |
| Phase 2 — Validating | $100–200/day (CBO) | Winners + 1% LLA from data | 2.5–3x |
| Phase 3 — Scaling | $300–1,000+/day (CBO) | Broad + LLA, proven creatives only | 3–3.5x |
| Phase 4 — Mature | $1,000+/day | Duplicate winning CBOs + retargeting layer | 3.5–4x (blended) |

**Scale rule:** Increase budget 20–30% every 48–72 hours if ROAS holds. Never more than 50% in one day (resets algorithm learning).

**Kill criteria:**
- 2x daily budget spent, zero purchases
- ROAS below 1.5 for 3 consecutive days after creative refresh
- CPP exceeds 60% of gross margin

## Email/SMS Flows

**Abandoned Cart (3-email series):**
- Email 1 (30 min): Soft reminder. No discount. Hero image. One-click back to cart. 45% of recovered revenue.
- Email 2 (24 hr): Social proof focus. 3 photo reviews. Still no discount.
- Email 3 (48 hr): Urgency + offer. 10% off with real deadline. Highest discount redemption.
- SMS: Fires 15 min after Email 1. Under 160 chars. One link. One action.

**Post-Purchase Flow:**
- Immediate: Order confirmation + specific shipping estimate. Reduces support tickets 40%.
- Day 3: "While you wait" — how to use product, build anticipation, soft cross-sell
- Day 7–10 (near delivery): "Almost there" + complementary product. 4–8% conversion to second purchase.
- Day 14 post-delivery: Review request + referral offer → feeds social proof engine

**Winback (60/90/120 day inactive):**
- Email 1: "We miss you" + bestseller showcase
- Email 2: 15% off + new arrivals
- Email 3: Final "last chance" 20% off → suppress after this

**Economics:** Top 1% generate 20–30% of total revenue from email/SMS, which is how they can run thinner front-end ROAS.

## Ad-Friendly Product Checklist

- [ ] Understood within 5 seconds of watching — no explanation required
- [ ] Visual demonstration: transforms, moves, cleans, lights up, reveals
- [ ] Clear before/after state change (dirty→clean, pain→relief, cluttered→organized)
- [ ] Problem is universally recognizable without context (back pain, poor sleep, cable clutter)
- [ ] Novel form factor solving familiar problem ("why didn't this exist before")
- [ ] Scroll-stopping frame 1: unusual color, unexpected action, or satisfying sound
- [ ] Sellable from 5+ different angles (extends creative runway from weeks to months)

## What Separates Top 1%

1. **Speed**: 48–72 hours from product idea to live ads (vs. 2 weeks average). Product windows are 60–90 days before saturation.
2. **Testing discipline**: Kill at $50 with no ATC, no emotion. Run 5–10 product tests simultaneously. Hit rate: 1-in-8 to 1-in-12 becomes profitable.
3. **Creative volume**: 20–30 new creatives per month per winning product. 10–15 UGC creators on retainer.
4. **Supplier relationships**: Private CJ/sourcing agents = $1–2 lower COGS, priority fulfillment, custom packaging. At 500 orders/day, $1.50 COGS reduction = $22,500/month.
5. **Systems**: SOPs for every decision. $100K/month operation = 2–4 people. Average dropshipper doing same revenue works 80-hour weeks alone.
6. **Backend revenue**: Email/SMS list is the real asset. Front-end ads can run thin because backend covers margin.
7. **Exit discipline**: Already testing replacements before the current winner dies. The business is the testing system, not any product.
