## 💡 PRO TIPS FOR MAXIMUM RESULTS

### 1. Match Template to Audience Maturity
- **Cold Audience:** Use problem-solution templates (Slack, Notion style)
- **Warm Audience:** Use social proof templates (Brooklinen, Bombas style)
- **Hot Audience:** Use urgency templates (Chubbies, flash sale style)

### 2. Platform-Specific Best Practices
- **Meta/Facebook:** Professional quality, clear value prop, social proof
- **TikTok:** Authentic UGC, trending audio, under 15 seconds
- **Instagram:** Aesthetic consistency, stories for engagement, feed for brand
- **LinkedIn:** Professional tone, business outcomes, data-driven

### 3. A/B Test Template Styles
Don't just pick one! Test:
- Minimalist vs Bold
- Lifestyle vs Product Focus
- Before/After vs Feature Highlight
- UGC vs Professional

### 4. Seasonal Adjustments
- **Q4 Holidays:** Gift guides, urgency, bundles
- **January:** Transformation, new year goals
- **Summer:** Lifestyle, outdoor, vacation
- **Back-to-School:** Productivity, organization

### 5. Creative Fatigue Management
- Rotate templates every 2-3 weeks
- Keep winning formats, refresh creative execution
- Test new templates monthly
- Monitor frequency caps

## 🚨 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1. Copying Too Literally
❌ **Wrong:** "Ditch the bulky wallet" (exact Ridge copy)
✅ **Right:** "Ditch the bulky [your category]" (adapted to your product)

### 2. Ignoring Design Specs
❌ **Wrong:** Using bright pink when template says black (changes perception)
✅ **Right:** Following color psychology (black = premium, bright = fun/urgent)

### 3. Skipping the Testing Phase
❌ **Wrong:** Launching one template with full budget
✅ **Right:** Testing 3-5 templates with small budgets first

### 4. Forgetting Platform Context
❌ **Wrong:** Using same creative across Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn
✅ **Right:** Adapting template to platform norms

### 5. No Creative Refresh
❌ **Wrong:** Running same creative for months
✅ **Right:** Refreshing every 2-3 weeks to avoid fatigue
